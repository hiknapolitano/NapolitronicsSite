Henrique Napolitano Gonçalves

Game Developer


BA in Image and Sound (UFSCar), passionate about video games, music and technology. Worked at iMax Games as a Unity Developer for 2 years, also developed many personal projects from scratch and published them on Play Store. Always focusing on the final product: fun gameplay, good looking visuals and immersive and pleasant music and SFX.


Dicy Master Puzzle


2D Minimalist puzzle game, with about 80 different levels, divided in 4 “worlds”, progressively unlocked by the player. The objective is to leave only one remaining die, which can get really tricky as the levels progress. Each world adds a new special die which is necessary to the completion of the level.

Gravity Ball


Hypercasual mobile game with neon art style. You have to control a ball and prevent it from getting smashed or falling off the screen boundaries. The player can collect coins, used to buy different balls in the shopping menu. The platforms and obstacles are procedurally generated and the difficulty increases with the time the player “survives”.

Unrelated Pic Puzzle


Casual puzzle-quiz mobile game in which you have to select one among four pictures, before the time runs out. 3 of them always have something in common, and there’s only one that is not related to them. You have to think really fast and choose that one. The levels are randomly generated. It also has integration with Google Play Game Services, and features a button to share your score with your friends on social media.

Bookie Clicker


Idle-clicker relaxing Windows/browser game with pixel-art style. Made within a week for a game jam hosted online.

Tanks and Dungeons


3D combat game where you control a tank inside celestial dungeons. This is a work in progress, not a finished game. The audio was implemented through Wwise, a middleware that allows for many perks when working with sounds and music in complex projects.

Ambition


Minimalist casual arcade game. For each item collected by the player, a bullet gets shot in the direction of the player’s current movement, making the game more and more difficult as the player’s score gets higher. The final goal is reaching a score of 20. It's like the classic game "Snake", but with a twist.
